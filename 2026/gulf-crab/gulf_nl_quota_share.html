Gulf vs Newfoundland Share of Canadian Snow Crab Production
Total allowable catch (tonnes) and Gulf share %, 2016–2026E
Gulf quota peaked at ~43,500 tonnes in 2017 and will fall to ~16,300 tonnes in 2026 — a 63% decline. Since the 2023 high of ~35,800 tonnes, the drop has been sharp: down 55% in three years. Gulf's share of total Canadian production fell from 46% in 2017 to under 19% in 2025-26. The buyers who need Gulf-origin crab now compete for a scarcer fraction of a shrinking total, which is the structural driver of the expanding Gulf premium.
Source: Seafood Datasearch from DFO quota announcements and industry estimates
