Key Insights

  • US exports to Canada peak during July-October, coinciding with the Maine lobster season
  • There is a negative correlation (-0.29) between US exports to Canada and inventory levels during July-October
  • This suggests lobsters from Maine are being processed in Canada rather than held in inventory
  • Monthly inventory patterns remain the strongest predictor
  • For summer months (July-Oct), considering US exports to Canada improves inventory prediction by ~9%
  • August and September show moderate positive correlations (0.47) between inventory and US exports to Canada within each month

About This Visualization

This dashboard displays analysis of lobster inventory data from 2005 to 2016, with a focus on understanding seasonal patterns and the relationship between US exports to Canada and inventory levels.

The data shows that inventory has seasonal peaks in June-July and December-January, while US exports to Canada peak during the Maine lobster season (July-October).

You can explore different views to understand these relationships better and examine correlations between inventory and various trade metrics.

Units: Inventory is measured in million pounds. Exports and landings are measured in metric tons (MT).

Data Source: Source of data is Lobster_Master file maintained by Seafood Datasearch.
